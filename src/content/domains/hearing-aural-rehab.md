SLPs working with individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing focus on auditory skill development, spoken language, and communication strategy training. This domain often involves close collaboration with audiologists.

## LLM Strengths in This Domain

- Structuring auditory training hierarchies
- Drafting parent education about hearing aids, cochlear implants, or assistive listening devices
- Organizing session notes that track auditory skill progression
- Brainstorming functional listening goals

## LLM Limitations

- Cannot interpret audiograms or hearing aid settings
- Cannot assess auditory perception or discrimination skills
- May not understand the progression of auditory skill development (detection → discrimination → identification → comprehension)
- Cannot account for individual device settings or mapping

## Key Considerations

- Collaborate closely with audiology — the SLP role is communication, not device management
- Goals should reflect functional listening in real-world environments
- Document aided vs. unaided performance when relevant
