## The Prompt

```
I am an SLP planning therapy activities. Generate 3-5 therapy activities for the following: target skill (I will specify), age range (I will specify), and setting (school, private practice, or early intervention). For each activity, include: the activity name, materials needed, step-by-step procedure, how to take data during the activity, and at least one way to increase or decrease difficulty. Activities should be functional, motivating for the age group, and practical with common therapy materials. Do not suggest apps or proprietary programs. Do not generate activities that require materials I would not reasonably have access to in my setting.
```

## Why This Works

- **Constrained by three variables** — specifying skill, age, and setting produces activities that are immediately usable rather than generic
- **Built-in data collection** — every activity includes a data collection method, reinforcing evidence-based practice
- **Scaffolding included** — increase/decrease difficulty options let you adjust in the moment without needing a new plan
- **Setting-realistic materials** — "materials I would reasonably have" prevents suggestions that require expensive or specialized equipment
- **No proprietary dependencies** — avoiding app and program recommendations keeps activities accessible regardless of budget

## When to Use

During session planning when you need fresh activity ideas for a specific skill target. Especially useful when you are seeing multiple students with similar goals and need variety, or when working with a new population and need a starting point.

## When NOT to Use

- Do not use as a replacement for clinical reasoning about what skill to target — choose the target first, then generate activities
- Do not assume generated activities are evidence-based — verify the therapeutic approach aligns with current research
- Do not use activities without adapting them to the individual client's interests and abilities
