SNF and rehab SLPs balance functional outcomes, insurance justification, and productivity requirements. LLMs can help with the documentation burden — but beware of padding notes with impressive-sounding language that doesn't reflect actual clinical work.

## Key Considerations

- Goals must justify continued services to insurance reviewers
- Functional outcomes must be measurable and tied to daily living
- MDS documentation has specific requirements
- Discharge planning requires clear status summaries and recommendations
- Productivity pressure can tempt over-reliance on AI-generated language

## Red Flags Specific to This Setting

- Using AI to inflate documentation to meet productivity metrics
- Letting polished language mask lack of meaningful progress
- Generic functional goals that don't reflect the individual patient
- AI-generated "improvement" narratives that don't match actual data
